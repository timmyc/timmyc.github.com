Make Time

Sep 20th, 2012

I have caught myself a few times lately, staring at my daughters, mouth agape, in wonder an awe about how quickly they are growing up.  Time marches past us all with no regard to how much fun we are currently having - and like Ferris said life moves pretty fast.  You really need to stop and look around from time to time.

And it does move fast.  A month ago marked the 1st anniversarry of my cousin Pete’s passing.  Peter taught me many things about life, but the biggest of which is truly like that quote from the 80’s hit movie… life goes fast.

In the past year I have tried to make it a point, made it a goal in my life to stop and look around more often.  Watch my girls grow in front of my eyes.  Spend more time with those I love.  And spend more time doing the things I love.  It has been amazingly rewarding.

So with the summer break quickly running out of time, I decided to make some time, and “look around a little”.

Fishmaster in Redding

 The first trip I took was down to Redding, CA to help my cousin pack up for a big move back to Spokane.  Amanda is an amazing person who has been through so much in the past year, it was the very least I could do… and I was also really looking forward to hanging with her awesome kiddos too.

Time was made and spent doing fun things.  Watching Diary of A Wimpy Kid, hanging with neighbors at a pool party, garage saleing, reminicing about Pete, and of course fishing with Ethan.  Nothing is more magical than hanging out with a kid that is doing what they love.  Nothing.  Stop reading this blog post, and go out and do it, and be inspired. 

Backpacking Bear

I have been wanting to take Kaydee on her first “real” camping trip for quite sometime.  No trailer, no car camping, the real kind.  Hiking in, and enjoying nature.  So a few weekends ago we did just so.  Left home Friday afternoon, and went up to Little Three Creeks Lake.  The hike wasn’t long - under 2 miles in fact, but it didn’t matter.  We were camping.

My pack outweighed hers by 40lbs easily… but I would have been willing to carry 40 more for the experience.  Setting up the tent, mac n cheese in a bag, climbing rocks in an alpine meadow, and skpping stones next to a bubbling spring - all of these moments and many more are priceless.

As I write this though, some of those magical spots are now being burned in the Pole Creek fire - I guess it was good we made time to get out there when we did - a whole one day before the fire broke out.

 

So to the loyal blog readers, who actually read this far… Get out, make time, and do the things you love with the ones you love.  And be sure to write about it, and spread the smiles around to a world that can use more things to smile about.

Riviere Aux Chutes

Jun 3rd, 2012

Amazingly awesome weekend playing, in and around the Deschutes River.  Sometimes I can’t believe how lucky I am to walk out my front door, walk 5 minutes, and be fishing in such a stunning place.

My stoke only got higher when I landed my first fish (minnow?  it was like 5” long), and was equally as stoked when the second one (8 inches of pure furry) gulped up my parachute adams.  So fantastic to be waded out in the river, casting away.

Whole Wheat Banana Bread

Jun 1st, 2012

I have been trying some variations on a “Mom Classic” the past few weeks, and have finally refined the recipe down to something that is just waaaaay yummy. Teagan, in fact, just ate 3 pieces of this (thats a lot of fiber for a little one, bombs coming soon).

So here is the recipe as it currently stands:

* 1/2 Cup Organic Evaporated Cane Juice * 3 TSP Agave Nectar


* 2 cups (sifted first) Bob’s Red Mill Whole Wheat Flour
* 2 TSP Baking Soda
* 1/2 TSP Salt
* 2 Large Eggs
* 4 oz Organic Applesauce (no sugar added)
* 3 TSP Coconut Oil (melted)
* 4 Very Ripe Bananas (as my mom said this weekend, ripe ones are better than ones that have been in the freezer)
* 1 cup of Raisins

Preheat oven to 350, and grease/flour a bread pan.

1) In a large mixing bowl, cream together sugar, agave, applesauce, coconut oil, and eggs.

2) Sift together flour, salt, and baking soda.

3) And in another bowl, mash bananas.

4) Mix 1 cup sifted dry ingredients into the creamed sugar / oil mixture

5) Add 1/2 the mashed bananas, mix

6) Repeat steps 4 & 5 with remaining ingredients, and toss in Raisins and mix. Pour into greased/floured bread pan.

I bake the bread for 30 minutes at 350, then cover loosely with foil and bake for around another 20 minutes. Check with a toothpick and when it comes clean it is ready.

Memories and Dreams

May 31st, 2012

Memorial weekend most definitely conjured up memories for me.

Even though the weekend started out with an unexpected twist - Becky staying home due to a stomach bug - and was a quite busy one overall - there were a few moments where memories from the past came rushing back to me like a dream sequence.

When I first walked into my parents house, my eyes kept darting to the back door, looking for the pups there waiting to come in - or eagerly waiting for a handout from Grandma :).  This dream of a memory happened numerous times during my visit.

Sorrow of missing friends no longer around was quickly hidden away by the creation of wonderful new memories - ones that I’m sure someday will come back to me and spark similar nostalgia when I walk through those doors.

Memories of the smiles shared with grandparents, playing with cousins, and laughter and happiness.

Before I set out for the long drive home (360 miles with two kids is daunting), I walked back to a dreamscape.  I visited the meadow we often went to with our friends… though the grass was green and lush from the spring rains, my mind quickly turned it dry, and a breeze whisked through the tall grass.

I walked through the tall dry grass just like Chinook loved to do - felt it touch my skin and lived the memory as a dream all over again.  Big doggie smiles, loving the moment.  I missed them all: Becky, Sophie, and Chinook, but they were with me there in that moment.  In that dream.
