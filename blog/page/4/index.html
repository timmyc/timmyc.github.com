Rearview Mirror

Jan 2nd, 2012

Well in my mind I had planned a long and drawn out post reflecting on 2011.  There were some wonderful times, some bad times, some very very sad times, and some frustrating times.  But overall the past year which was filled with changes and challenges for put me in quite a wonderful place.  

Typically in the past I take the passing of a year as a time to look back and critique my steps that I have taken… but I don’t think that is really a productive process for me.  So instead, I’m just looking at 2011 in the rear view, admiring the good memories, and then looking ahead to what is around the next corner… I find this helps me stay focused on the now, and live the day to its fullest.

But, 2011 wanted to have one final laugh on my account this past Saturday.  Eager to make turns out at Brokentop I was thwarted by a frozen truck canopy (which Schralper peed on to unfreeze) and the sledneck gods tossed this one my way:

The sled made it out (Thanks Eric, Trever, Mike and Josh), but some pride was left behind.  But the story and the laughs I have already had about this made it worth it.

So to those of you who I shared memories with this year, thank you.  For those whom I didn’t, maybe our paths will cross in 2012.  And to my family, my lovely wife in particular, I’m very grateful and thankful for you all, and all the love you give me.  Without you, this year wouldn’t have been possible.

Powder Alerts by Text

Dec 21st, 2011

So I already blogged about this in another spot, but I feel this deserves a bit of airtime on my personal site as well.

I have been working on a personal side project the past few weeks… fueled by my love of skiing, and a desire to build something using Twilio’s awesome service.  The result of my work has been lurking online for a few weeks now, but I’m hoping by handing it out as a virtual Christmas presed to the shredders of Mt. Bachelor… it just might start to snow again.

So the concept is pretty simple.  You signup and tell the site what freshies threshold you want to be notified about.  It snows, you get a text, and off you go.

There are a few other features in the works around NOAA forecast data, and possibly the ability to setup powder morning wake up phone calls.  I’m not sure as of yet if this thing will catch on, and if it does, how to cover the costs… but I’m just putting it out there to see what happens.

So if you ski or ride, sign up, get stoked, and pray for snow.  Oh and click that big image up there to be taken to the signup page, or CLICK HERE.
