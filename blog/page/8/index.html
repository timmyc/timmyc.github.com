The Lips Live at South Sister

Jul 15th, 2011

Sometimes I have awesome dreams, and last night… or I suppose this morning I had an epic dream. It is always fascinating to trace back roots of a dream to reality, and this one had its genesis in my excitement over purchasing tickets for the flaming lips in portland and perhaps talking to my buddy Josh on the phone yesterday. Anyhow, here it goes.

In The Van

The first part of the dream that I can remember is that I was in a van with Josh, his wife Melanie, some other girl whom I don’t remember who it was and the magical Wayne Coyne, front man of the Flaming Lips.

We were on our way to skin to South Sister, where the Flaming Lips were performing a concert that evening…. you know because there are big rock shows all the time on the plateau of South Sister… with the abundance of power and parking… anyhow, go with it.

Wayne had never skiied before, but we all assured him it was really easy to do. In my dream thought process I was thinking… oh crap Wayne is a total gaper, and it might take too long to skin in, and he won’t get there in time for the show… but I kept my mouth shut.

I remember having a serious bromance crush on Wayne in the dream, kept resisting urges to snap pics with him and I on the phone and post on the interwebs. My van-mates didn’t seem to really care that Wayne was there.

Magical Chair Lift

So we arrive at South Sister, and instead of skiing we decide to take the chair lift (!! oddly this isn’t my first dream with lift-served access of South Sister !!) up to the concert venue. There was a big crowd of others boarding the chair lift, and everyone was like OMG ITS WAYNE. Uber.

Unique Show

Anyone who has ever been to a Lips concert knows they are fairly interesting productions. The South Sister show though was more like a cross between a rock show and a vaudeville production. The crowd wasn’t huge, but there was lots of crazy stuff going on… and I was tapped to play the role of the mad hatter in the production:

The opening song was Enthusiasm For Life Defeats Existential Fear

I think I woke up shortly after the song was over. What does it all mean? I’m pretty sure it means I need to quit my job, and go on tour with the lips.

Crooked Cravings

Jul 10th, 2011

It All Began With A Fail

Yesterday while at the local ‘hole I had a fail of epic proportions. I was casting my hand-me-down Ugly Stick, and the tip flew off into the water. Unfortunately my lure didn’t allow me to reel half my pole back in, and well, I went home with half a pole. Awesome.

So this morning I found myself with a new, smaller, lighter Ugly Stick… so the only thing I could do was get out there and break it in.

Never Been

Living in Central Oregon still amazes me. I have been here over ten years now, and I had never been to the Crooked River gorge below the Bowman damn. I took some beta from a co-worker and headed out there this morning.

The canyon is beautiful, and the fishing holes and easy access are abundant. I pulled off at the Poison Butte campground and began to cast away.

The river is fairly wide, and was not running very deep during my visit, so finding quality holes did take some work…. but when I found one, the new pole got broken-in in fine fashion.

The One That Didn’t Get Away

The first trout caught with the new pole is the largest I have ever caught. The beauty filled up my entire net (front-to-back) and still folded up a bit in the tail. Since the net is 14” long, I’d put this puppy at an easy 16”. It was an amazing experience to put up with the fight, and finally land it in the net. I was shaking with excitement, and unfortunately didn’t have my camera handy.

I was able to land one more great rainbow, about 8” in length (pictured), and had three more fish on, but they were able to wiggle off by doing some amazing jumps for me out of the water.

Besides the amazing fish, and the wonderful views… the best part of the day was only losing 1 lure… a new record for me.

I can’t wait to get back out there to the Crooked again!
