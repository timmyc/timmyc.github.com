Make Time

Sep 20th, 2012

I have caught myself a few times lately, staring at my daughters, mouth agape, in wonder an awe about how quickly they are growing up.  Time marches past us all with no regard to how much fun we are currently having - and like Ferris said life moves pretty fast.  You really need to stop and look around from time to time.

And it does move fast.  A month ago marked the 1st anniversarry of my cousin Pete’s passing.  Peter taught me many things about life, but the biggest of which is truly like that quote from the 80’s hit movie… life goes fast.

In the past year I have tried to make it a point, made it a goal in my life to stop and look around more often.  Watch my girls grow in front of my eyes.  Spend more time with those I love.  And spend more time doing the things I love.  It has been amazingly rewarding.

So with the summer break quickly running out of time, I decided to make some time, and “look around a little”.

Fishmaster in Redding

 The first trip I took was down to Redding, CA to help my cousin pack up for a big move back to Spokane.  Amanda is an amazing person who has been through so much in the past year, it was the very least I could do… and I was also really looking forward to hanging with her awesome kiddos too.

Time was made and spent doing fun things.  Watching Diary of A Wimpy Kid, hanging with neighbors at a pool party, garage saleing, reminicing about Pete, and of course fishing with Ethan.  Nothing is more magical than hanging out with a kid that is doing what they love.  Nothing.  Stop reading this blog post, and go out and do it, and be inspired. 

Backpacking Bear

I have been wanting to take Kaydee on her first “real” camping trip for quite sometime.  No trailer, no car camping, the real kind.  Hiking in, and enjoying nature.  So a few weekends ago we did just so.  Left home Friday afternoon, and went up to Little Three Creeks Lake.  The hike wasn’t long - under 2 miles in fact, but it didn’t matter.  We were camping.

My pack outweighed hers by 40lbs easily… but I would have been willing to carry 40 more for the experience.  Setting up the tent, mac n cheese in a bag, climbing rocks in an alpine meadow, and skpping stones next to a bubbling spring - all of these moments and many more are priceless.

As I write this though, some of those magical spots are now being burned in the Pole Creek fire - I guess it was good we made time to get out there when we did - a whole one day before the fire broke out.

 

So to the loyal blog readers, who actually read this far… Get out, make time, and do the things you love with the ones you love.  And be sure to write about it, and spread the smiles around to a world that can use more things to smile about.
