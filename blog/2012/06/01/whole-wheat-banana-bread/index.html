Whole Wheat Banana Bread

Jun 1st, 2012

I have been trying some variations on a “Mom Classic” the past few weeks, and have finally refined the recipe down to something that is just waaaaay yummy. Teagan, in fact, just ate 3 pieces of this (thats a lot of fiber for a little one, bombs coming soon).

So here is the recipe as it currently stands:

* 1/2 Cup Organic Evaporated Cane Juice * 3 TSP Agave Nectar


* 2 cups (sifted first) Bob’s Red Mill Whole Wheat Flour
* 2 TSP Baking Soda
* 1/2 TSP Salt
* 2 Large Eggs
* 4 oz Organic Applesauce (no sugar added)
* 3 TSP Coconut Oil (melted)
* 4 Very Ripe Bananas (as my mom said this weekend, ripe ones are better than ones that have been in the freezer)
* 1 cup of Raisins

Preheat oven to 350, and grease/flour a bread pan.

1) In a large mixing bowl, cream together sugar, agave, applesauce, coconut oil, and eggs.

2) Sift together flour, salt, and baking soda.

3) And in another bowl, mash bananas.

4) Mix 1 cup sifted dry ingredients into the creamed sugar / oil mixture

5) Add 1/2 the mashed bananas, mix

6) Repeat steps 4 & 5 with remaining ingredients, and toss in Raisins and mix. Pour into greased/floured bread pan.

I bake the bread for 30 minutes at 350, then cover loosely with foil and bake for around another 20 minutes. Check with a toothpick and when it comes clean it is ready.
